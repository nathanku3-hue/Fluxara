# Architectural & Product Decision Log - Fluxara

This log documents the historical decisions made during the design and development of Fluxara, capturing the context, options considered, and selected paths.

---

## Decision D001: Convex Optimizer vs. Nonlinear Physics Decoupling
- **Date:** 2026-06-10
- **Status:** Approved
- **Context:** Modeling silicon fatigue (Coffin-Manson empirical equation) and high-temperature creep involves fractional exponents and highly nonlinear equations. Solving these non-convex models in real time violates the sub-100ms latency requirement of control planes.
- **Options Considered:**
  1. **Strict Nonlinear MPC:** Run a full nonlinear programming solver (e.g., IPOPT or SNOPT). *Rejected: Too slow, prone to local minima.*
  2. **Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP):** Discretize the fatigue curves into piecewise-linear steps. *Rejected: Increases computational complexity.*
  3. **Convex/Nonlinear Decoupling (Selected):** Use a convex quadratic setpoint ramp penalty in the solver (`solver.py`) as a proxy for fatigue, and calculate the true nonlinear Coffin-Manson damage ledger within the 1-second simulation step in the environment (`env.py`).
- **Rationale:** Keeps the solver step strictly convex (Quadratic Program), allowing sub-millisecond execution times with `OSQP` while maintaining an accurate physical record of silicon degradation.

## Decision D002: Multi-Rate Clock Scheme (1s vs. 300s)
- **Date:** 2026-06-10
- **Status:** Approved
- **Context:** Grid pricing updates every 5 minutes, but data center temperatures and checkpoint processes change in seconds. A 1-second MPC lookahead horizon over 24 hours would result in $86,400$ variables, making optimization intractable.
- **Options Considered:**
  1. **Single-Rate 1-second Loop:** Run both solver and environment at 1-second resolution. *Rejected: Optimizer matrix dimension size explodes.*
  2. **Single-Rate 5-minute Loop:** Run both solver and environment at 300-second resolution. *Rejected: Racks can overheat in seconds, rendering the safety checks useless.*
  3. **Multi-Rate Scheme (Selected):** Step the environment forward at 1-second intervals (integrating physical differential equations) and invoke the solver MPC every 300 seconds to set caps.
- **Rationale:** Captures the fast micro-second and second-level transient thermal dynamics while keeping the optimization horizon computationally small (12 steps of 5 minutes = 1 hour lookahead).

## Decision D003: Continuous Relaxation of IT Actions
- **Date:** 2026-06-10
- **Status:** Approved
- **Context:** Checkpointing or pausing a distributed training run is a binary decision ($0$ or $1$). Ingesting binary decisions into the MPC creates a Mixed-Integer Quadratic Program (MIQP), which is NP-hard.
- **Options Considered:**
  1. **Strict Mixed-Integer Programming:** Model checkpointing as binary variables and use a branch-and-bound solver (e.g., GLPK, CBC). *Rejected: Computationally heavy and prone to timeouts.*
  2. **Continuous Relaxation (Selected):** Relax checkpointing and job-pausing into continuous effort variables $c_t \in [0.0, 1.0]$. Penalize the checkpoint effort quadratically ($b \sum c_t^2$) to simulate network bandwidth congestion.
- **Rationale:** Maintains optimization convexity. The continuous setpoints generated by the MPC are translated into discrete job-level action schedules in a second-stage rounding/dispatch layer.

## Decision D004: Ingestion Strategy and Testing Fallbacks
- **Date:** 2026-06-10
- **Status:** Approved
- **Context:** Integrating real CAISO grid data APIs and telemetry stream collectors introduces external network dependencies, making local testing difficult.
- **Options Considered:**
  1. **API Dependency Only:** Hard-depend on wattTime, CAISO, and external telemetry agents. *Rejected: Slower testing loop, fragile.*
  2. **Synthetic Fixtures with Ingestion Hooks (Selected):** Build the environment to accept pandas DataFrames (`lmp_df`, `gpu_trace_df`), but generate synthetic daily LMP and carbon profiles by default.
- **Rationale:** Ensures developers can clone the repo and run `python fluxara_core/demo.py` instantly without external API keys, while allowing production deployments to swap in real data via DataFrames.

## Decision D005: Release Prioritization (Round 8 Roadmap Split)
- **Date:** 2026-06-10
- **Status:** Approved
- **Context:** Evaluating the post-MVP roadmap features: stochastic bidding, compiler sub-graph routing, and Landauer reversible computing.
- **Options Considered:**
  1. **Parallel Development:** Develop all three tracks at once. *Rejected: Stretches resources, slows time-to-market.*
  2. **Prioritized Track Allocation (Selected):**
     - **v0.2:** Fast-track **Stochastic Bidding** (R8-1) to secure the bidding surface against adversarial market probing.
     - **v0.3:** Develop **Aging-Aware Sub-graph Routing** (R8-2) as the next compiler-integration step.
     - **v0.R:** Move **Landauer Arbitrage** (R8-3) to a long-horizon research track.
- **Rationale:** Current accelerators do not support physical reversible execution modes, making Landauer arbitrage a theoretical research topic. Protecting the control plane from market adversaries and scheduling around silicon wear-out have immediate commercial payouts.
